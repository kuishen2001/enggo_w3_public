AP Chemistry 2023 FRQ Q1 Solution

(a)(i)

The complete electron configuration for an Mn atom in the ground state is 1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d5 4s2 or [Ar] 3d5 4s2.

(a)(ii)

When manganese forms cations, electrons are lost from the 4s subshell.

(b)

Mn = 61.262 g - 60.169 g = 1.093 g

(c)

Cl = 62.673 g - 61.262 g = 1.411 g = 1.411 g * 1 mol / 35.45 g = 0.03980 mol

(d)

Mn:Cl = 0.0199 mol : 0.0398 mol = 1:2, so the empirical formula is MnCl2.

(e)

The number of moles of Cl calculated for this trial will be less than the number calculated in part (c), because the MnxCly that splatters out of the beaker will reduce the mass remains in the beaker as it is heated to dryness and thus reduce the amount calculated for Cl.

(f)(i)

The balanced net ionic equation for the reaction that has the greatest thermodynamic favorability is: 2MnO2(s) + Zn(s) -> Mn2O3(s) + ZnO(s).

(f)(ii)

E cell = 0.15 V - (-1.28 V) = 1.43 V

(f)(iii)

DeltaG = - nFE = - 2 * 96485.3321 s A/mol * 1.43 V * KJ / 1000 J = 276 KJ/mol
